Dashes

Hyphen: Regular-price and other instances where two words are tied together.

En-dash: Ranges like 1‐2 and Thursday–Friday.

Em-dash: Pauses within a sentence—specifically to discuss a separate but related thought.

Quotes and apostrophes

Use typographer’s quotes (curly quotes) around something someone has said: “This is a quote”.

Use single curly apostrophes for contractions (can’t) and possessives (someone’s). Plurals do not have apostrophes.

Use straight quotation marks for measurements: 5'6".

One word or two? Hyphenation?

Regular-price and regular-priced items are sold at regular price.

Standalone systems stand alone.

In-store offers are only available in store.

Multifunction is one word.

A lot is one word. An alot is a functional animal used to illustrate grammatical principles.

All-purpose products serve all purposes.

Typesetting

Never separate brand names, instutition names, or other multi-word proper nouns with line breaks. Use &nbsp; to avoid this.

Avoid widowed lines at the top of the next text frame.

Don’t orphan lines at the bottom of the previous text frame.

Use non-breaking spaces to avoid runt words.

Check text blocks for rivers of whitespace flowing between the words.

Numbers

Spell out numbers below 10 unless it includes a fraction.

Units of measurement do not have spaces following the numbers: 1lb.
