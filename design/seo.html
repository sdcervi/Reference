The basics

Search engine optimization (SEO) is a set of techniques, concepts, and principles to help you improve your site’s ranking in search engine results when people search for keywords related to your site. Simply put, the main concept of SEO is discovering which keywords people are likely to search for when looking for the type of content your site has, and then using these keywords in the right places on your site to increase its relevance score and therefore place your site higher in the list of search results.

The first result receives the highest click-through rate (CTR), and CTRs drop off significantly past the top three results. Clicks bottom out after ranking position 10; 95% of users never look past the first page of results.

Ranking algorithms look at several factors to determine a site’s rank:

  • Keyword relevance: how directly relevant the keywords on your site are to the user’s search keywords.
  • Keyword precision: whether your keywords are targeted at a specific topic, or you’ve simply spammed common keywords across your whole site.
  • Incoming links: how many other sites link to your site, and how relevant those links are. The more authoritative and higher-ranking a site linking to yours is, the more weight its inbound link will have on your own ranking. (This can be abused with link farm sites that consist entirely of pages full of links to other sites. That’s why relevance between your site and those that link to yours is factored in.)
  • Update frequency: sites that regularly publish new, relevant, and high-quality content will often rank higher.
  • HTTPS status: whether your site is served over HTTPS and is secure. Non-secured websites will often rank lower or be excluded completely.
  • Loading speed: sites that load quickly, especially on mobile, will rank higher.
  • Mobile friendliness: sites that are designed to work well on mobile are significantly prioritized.
  • Bounce rate and dwell time: if users go to your site but leave quickly, that’s a high bounce rate with a low dwell time, and will lower your overall ranking.
  • CTR: if a site’s CTR improves, its position in the rankings will also improve.
  • Accessibility: sites that comply with WCAG 2.0 accessibility guidelines will have a higher rank. This includes useful alt text, screen reader friendly links and headings, and sufficient color contrast.
  • Domain name age: the majority of top-ten Google search result rankings are three years old or older. Simply giving your site time to age will help.
  • Domain name content: exact-match domain names that contain relevant keywords can help, provided your site doesn’t fit the profile of a spam site or link farm. Example: cookingforone.com for recipes designed for one person. However, if choosing between an exact-match domain and a recognizable brand name, go with the brand name and focus on optimizing the site itself.
  • Search intent: try to understand what users are actually searching for when they use specific keywords. Keywords will change depending on whether they want to:
    • Find a particular website (navigational)
    • Get the answer to a question (informational)
    • Research information before making a purchase (investigational)
    • Make a purchase (transactional)
    Properly optimized sites will include content for each of these search types.
  • Content length: longer pages with more content will be ranked higher, within reason; don’t pad your word count unnecessarily just to improve ranking, as this decreases the total content quality.
  • Page structure: set up your pages with relevant headings, properly tagged within the h tag hierarchy (h1, h2, etc.). Make sure each page only has one h1 tag, and that it contains the title of the page.
  • Meta descriptions: these will show up as a site’s preview text in most search engines, when specified. Limit these to 155 characters. More information on meta tags »
  • Social media shares: the more people share your content on social media, the higher your site will rank in search results.
  • Business information: if your site includes business contact details such as name, address, and phone number, has business listings on Google My Business and Facebook, has reviews linked to it, and uses local search terms if serving a local population, that will improve rankings as well.

Types of keywords

There are many categories of keywords used in SEO; each is used to target different audiences at different stages of search.

Terms:

  • Conversion rate: the percentage of users that complete the desired goal of the site, whether that’s purchasing a product, subscribing to a service, or something else.
  • Vertical: a particular niche of products, content, or services. These are typically narrow markets whose specific needs makes them more likely to buy your products or services, or consume your content.

Off-page techniques

The best way to improve your SEO ranking with external factors is to make sure that a lot of relevant, reputable, and high-ranking sites link to yours. Ideally, inbound links will come from sites with similar subject matters; for a site selling fish bait, an inbound link from an angling community is more important than a link from a hairdresser.

Posting about your content on social media, especially in a way that other users are likely to share and repost it, will help as well. But ultimately, one of the best ways to get more inbound links is just to give it time. Keep publishing quality content and sharing it via social media, build a loyal audience, and give that audience time to grow. As it grows, your inbound links will increase as well. Interact with your audience, join forums relevant to your content and interact with people there, and make sure to use usernames linked to your site (i.e. your brand name).

Paying for advertising, marketing, and influencer content is another way to drive up inbound links, traffic, and audience size, but can easily be abused and will be penalized by the algorithms.

Focus on getting inbound links from high-quality sources, rather than the total quantity of inbound links. One high-quality link will matter more than several low-quality links. Here are the most effective methods to improve quality and quantity of inbound links:

  • Social networking: engage with your social media audience and get them talking about your brand.
  • Commenting: leave meaningful and useful comments on blogs and social posts relevant to your content to increase your visibility.
  • Forums: join and participate in forums and subreddits relevant to your topic.
  • Influencer outreach: build relationships with reputable influencers in your topic area, ask for their feedback and insight, and work with them to publish it.
  • Guest authors: feature guest authors on your site, and guest write for others.
  • Social bookmarking: promote your content on sites like StumbleUpon, Digg, and Reddit in relevant communities.
  • Q&A: write thoughtful and useful answers on sites like Stack Overflow and Quora in your chosen topic. Link back to your site when there’s relevant content to share, just don’t overdo it.
  • Newsletters: curate newsletters with recently published content, some exclusive content, and relevant information into easy-to-digest and short emails. Publish them according to the local time of the largest slice of your audience, and avoid spamming them with emails.

Pleasing the audience or pleasing search engines

This depends on your site’s goal. Do you want to get your content in front of as many eyes as possible by using algorithms, or rely on crowd-sourced advertising by creating high quality content that your readers will share with new readers?

Never sacrifice user experience and readability for better SEO ranking. Annoying your audience will only decrease the metrics that determine your rank. Google will never be your paying customer. Your readers, however, might.
