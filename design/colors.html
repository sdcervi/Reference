Colorblindness in web design

“Colorblind” is itself a misnomer; a more correct term would be “color vision impairment.” However, it has a significant effect on web design color palettes by limiting the range of colors that provide effective contrast while still being visually pleasing.

Name Form of impairment How it affects vision
Protanope Absence of red retinal photoreceptors
  • Difficuly distinguishing colors in the green-yellow-red area of the spectrum
  • Brightness of red, orange, and yellow colors is reduced
Deuteranope Absence of green retinal photoreceptors
  • Difficuly distinguishing colors in the green-yellow-red area of the spectrum
Tritanope Absence of blue retinal photoreceptors
  • Difficult distinguishing blue from green, and yellow from violet

Choosing accessible color palettes

When selecting a color palette for a design, try to go for effective contrast using hue, saturation, lightness, complimentary colors, etc. that is still visible even when color vision impaired.
